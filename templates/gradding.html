Grade Home

Standardised grading of sustainable home through transparent criteria.

Constructed Building

Criteria

  • Energy Efficiency (20 points)

    1. Are energy-efficient lighting fixtures and appliances installed in your home?
    2. Is your home well-insulated to reduce energy consumption?
    3. Are there any available option of energy source from renewable energy resources in your home(Roof Top Solar Plant or Bio Gas Plant)?

  • Water Conservation (15 points)

    1. Are low-flow fixtures and fittings installed to reduce water usage?
    2. Do you have a rainwater harvesting system in place to conserve water?
    3. Are there any other water conservation strategies employed in your home?

  • Waste Management (10 points)

    1. Are there any recycling or composting programs in place? are there any options of utilizing waste for energy generation(Bio Gas Plant)?
    2. Are there any measures in place to reduce waste and promote sustainability in your home?

  • Indoor Environment Quality (15 points)

    1. Are low-emission materials used to reduce air pollution indoors?
    2. Is your home well-ventilated to promote good indoor air quality?
    3. Is there a program in place to ensure the regular maintenance and cleaning of heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems?

  • Materials (15 points)

    1. Were sustainable materials used in the construction of your home?
    2. Were materials sourced locally to reduce transportation emissions?
    3. Were recycled or reused materials used to reduce waste and environmental impact?
    4. Are there any plans in place to replace any non-sustainable building materials with more sustainable options?

  • Transportation (15 points)

    1. Do you use biking or walking as transportation options?
    2. Do you use carpooling or ride-sharing programs for transportation?
    3. Is there public transportation readily accessible from your home?

  • Innovation (10 points)

    1. Are there any innovative strategies employed to promote sustainability in your home?
    2. Are there any new technologies or materials being used to reduce environmental impact?
    3. Are there any unique or creative solutions employed to overcome challenges related to building sustainability?

Under construction building

Criteria

  • Site Selection (10 points)

    1. Did the site selected for your building construction caused harm to the existing environment?
    2. Was the site evaluated for its impact on the environment and local community?
    3. Was the site selected to minimize environmental impact and promote sustainability?

  • Planning and Design (20 points)

    1. Was the building designed to promote sustainability and reduce environmental impact?
    2. Was the building designed to optimize the use of natural light and ventilation?
    3. Were passive solar design strategies employed to reduce energy consumption?

  • Water Conservation (10 points)

    1. What measures were taken to reduce water consumption in your building?
    2. Were low-flow fixtures and fittings installed to reduce water usage?
    3. Were rainwater harvesting and greywater recycling systems installed to conserve water?

  • Materials (10 points)
    1. Were sustainable materials used in the construction of your building?
    2. Were materials sourced locally to reduce transportation emissions?
    3. Were recycled or reused materials used to reduce waste and environmental impact?

  • Indoor Environment Quality (15 points)

    1. Were low-emission materials used to reduce air pollution indoors?
    2. Were thermal comfort and acoustics taken into consideration during the building design process?

  • Energy Efficiency (20 points)

    1. Were energy-efficient appliances and equipment installed?
    2. Were energy-efficient building materials and insulation used to reduce energy consumption?

  • Renewable Energy (5 points)

    1. Was renewable energy incorporated into your building's design?
    2. Were solar panels or other forms of renewable energy installed to generate electricity?
    3. Was the building designed to make use of natural light and reduce the need for artificial lighting?

  • Waste Management (10 points)

    1. How was waste management addressed during the construction of your building?
    2. Were measures taken to reduce construction waste and recycle materials?
    3. Were waste disposal systems installed to handle waste generated by building occupants?
